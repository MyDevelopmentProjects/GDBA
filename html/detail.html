the dialogue between public and private sectors
12 march 2017

On April 28, the Public-Private Dialogue (PPD) on the Gaps of the Insolvency System in Georgia and the Necessity of Reforming was held in the Parliament, in the framework of the USAID ,,Governing for Growth'' (G4G) in Georgia Project - ''Strengthening PPD Platform in Parliament''. The event was organized by Business and Economic Centre (BEC) together with and hosted by the Sector Economy and Economic Policy Committee of the Parliament of Georgia.

The importance of effective insolvency system and the best practices were presented by Insolvency Experts: Ms. Nana Amisulashvili and Ms. Ketevan Bashinuridze.

Private sector perspectives on the gaps of the Insolvency System in Georgia were presented by the: Association of Law Firms of Georgia; Georgian Distributors Business Association; Georgian Producers' Federation; Georgian Business Association (BAG), Investors Council Georgia and Tax Payers Union.
The Georgian Government's positions with respect to the necessity of reforming were presented by: Mr. Mikheil Sarjveladze, Deputy Minister of Justice; Mr. Nikoloz Gagua, Deputy Minister of Finance and Ms. Nino Javakhadze, Deputy Minister of Economy and Sustainable Development.

Discussion was attended by almost 60 participants from public, private and NGO sectors.
